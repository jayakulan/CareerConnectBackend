INTERVIEW PREPARATION AND CAREER ADVICE

=== INTERVIEW PREPARATION ===

BEFORE THE INTERVIEW:
1. Research the company thoroughly
   - Company mission and values
   - Recent news and achievements
   - Products and services
   - Competitors and market position
   - Company culture and reviews

2. Understand the role
   - Review job description carefully
   - Identify required skills and qualifications
   - Prepare examples matching requirements
   - Research typical responsibilities

3. Prepare your materials
   - Updated resume
   - Portfolio or work samples
   - List of references
   - Questions for interviewer
   - Notepad and pen

4. Practice common questions
   - Tell me about yourself
   - Why do you want this job?
   - What are your strengths/weaknesses?
   - Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
   - Why should we hire you?
   - Describe a challenging situation

5. Plan logistics
   - Confirm date, time, and location
   - Test video call setup if virtual
   - Plan route and parking
   - Arrive 10-15 minutes early
   - Dress appropriately for company culture

DURING THE INTERVIEW:
1. First impressions
   - Firm handshake
   - Maintain eye contact
   - Smile and show enthusiasm
   - Good posture
   - Professional attire

2. Communication skills
   - Listen carefully to questions
   - Take a moment to think before answering
   - Use STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
   - Provide specific examples
   - Be concise but thorough
   - Ask clarifying questions if needed

3. Behavioral questions
   - Prepare stories demonstrating key competencies
   - Focus on your specific contributions
   - Highlight problem-solving abilities
   - Show leadership and teamwork
   - Discuss lessons learned from failures

4. Technical questions (for technical roles)
   - Think aloud through problems
   - Ask clarifying questions
   - Explain your reasoning
   - Consider edge cases
   - Discuss trade-offs
   - Be honest about what you don't know

5. Questions to ask interviewer
   - What does success look like in this role?
   - What are the biggest challenges facing the team?
   - How would you describe the company culture?
   - What opportunities for growth exist?
   - What are the next steps in the process?

AFTER THE INTERVIEW:
1. Send thank-you email within 24 hours
2. Reiterate interest in position
3. Reference specific discussion points
4. Address any concerns raised
5. Follow up if no response within stated timeframe

=== COMMON INTERVIEW QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS ===

QUESTION: Tell me about yourself
APPROACH:
- Brief professional summary (2-3 minutes)
- Current role and key achievements
- Relevant background and experience
- Why you're interested in this opportunity
- Keep it professional, not personal

QUESTION: What are your strengths?
APPROACH:
- Choose 2-3 relevant strengths
- Provide specific examples
- Relate to job requirements
- Show self-awareness
- Demonstrate value to employer

QUESTION: What are your weaknesses?
APPROACH:
- Choose real but not critical weakness
- Explain steps you're taking to improve
- Show self-awareness and growth mindset
- Don't say "I'm a perfectionist"
- Keep it brief and pivot to strengths

QUESTION: Why do you want to work here?
APPROACH:
- Show knowledge of company
- Align with company values
- Discuss specific aspects that appeal to you
- Connect to your career goals
- Express genuine enthusiasm

QUESTION: Why are you leaving your current job?
APPROACH:
- Stay positive about current employer
- Focus on seeking new opportunities
- Discuss career growth
- Emphasize what you're looking for
- Never badmouth previous employers

QUESTION: Describe a challenging situation
APPROACH:
- Use STAR method
- Choose relevant example
- Focus on your actions
- Highlight problem-solving skills
- Discuss positive outcome and lessons learned

QUESTION: Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
APPROACH:
- Show ambition and growth mindset
- Align with potential career path at company
- Demonstrate commitment
- Be realistic but aspirational
- Focus on skills development

=== SALARY NEGOTIATION ===

PREPARATION:
1. Research market rates
   - Use Glassdoor, PayScale, LinkedIn Salary
   - Consider location, experience, industry
   - Know your worth

2. Determine your range
   - Minimum acceptable salary
   - Target salary
   - Ideal salary
   - Consider total compensation

3. Timing
   - Wait for offer before discussing salary
   - Don't bring up salary in first interview
   - Let employer make first offer when possible

NEGOTIATION STRATEGIES:
1. Express enthusiasm for role
2. Thank them for the offer
3. Ask for time to review (24-48 hours)
4. Present your case with data
5. Discuss total compensation package
6. Be prepared to justify your request
7. Consider non-salary benefits
8. Get final offer in writing

WHAT TO NEGOTIATE:
- Base salary
- Signing bonus
- Performance bonuses
- Stock options/equity
- Vacation time
- Remote work flexibility
- Professional development budget
- Relocation assistance
- Start date

=== JOB SEARCH STRATEGIES ===

EFFECTIVE JOB SEARCH:
1. Define target roles and companies
2. Optimize LinkedIn profile
3. Customize resume for each application
4. Write tailored cover letters
5. Use multiple job boards
6. Leverage your network
7. Follow up on applications
8. Track applications in spreadsheet

JOB BOARDS AND RESOURCES:
- General: LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor, Monster
- Tech: Stack Overflow Jobs, GitHub Jobs, AngelList, Dice
- Remote: We Work Remotely, Remote.co, FlexJobs
- Startups: AngelList, Crunchbase, VentureLoop
- Executive: The Ladders, ExecuNet
- Industry-specific boards

LINKEDIN OPTIMIZATION:
1. Professional profile photo
2. Compelling headline
3. Detailed summary
4. Complete work history
5. Skills and endorsements
6. Recommendations
7. Engage with content
8. Join relevant groups
9. Set to "Open to Work"

APPLICATION BEST PRACTICES:
1. Apply within first 24-48 hours of posting
2. Follow application instructions exactly
3. Use keywords from job description
4. Proofread everything
5. Include cover letter unless stated otherwise
6. Follow up after 1-2 weeks
7. Keep applying to multiple positions
8. Don't get discouraged by rejections
